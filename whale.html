Here Are Some Information About Whales:

Size

Whales range in size from the dwarf sperm whale, which is 8.5 ft long and weighs 298 lb, to the blue whale, which is 98 ft long and weighs 210 short tons. The blue whale is the largest animal to have ever lived.

Teeth

Baleen whales have no teeth, but instead have baleen plates that help them filter out water while feeding on plankton and krill. Toothed whales have conical teeth that help them catch fish and squid.

Sperm Whales

Sperm whales have the largest brains on Earth, weighing up to 20 lbs and taking up to a third of their body length. They also have a large cavity in their heads filled with spermaceti oil, which is used for echolocation and was once used by whalers to make candles, creams, and ointments.

Span

The average lifespan of a blue whale is 70–90 years, while a humpback whale lives for about 45–50 years. The bowhead whale can live up to 200 years, making it one of the longest-living mammals on Earth.

Breathing

Whales are mammals, so they have lungs and breathe air at the surface of the water. They breathe through nostrils on top of their heads called blowholes.

Communication

Whales use a variety of sounds to communicate with each other. Humpback whales are known for their distinctive songs and acrobatic breaches.

Blubber

Whales have a thick layer of fat called blubber that insulates them from the cold ocean waters.

Hippos

Hippos are close cousins of whales.

Killer Whales

Killer whales, also known as orcas, are the largest members of the dolphin family.

about us

Some sea life, called rift communities, live around cracks in the ocean floor called fissures. These cracks expose hot minerals from the Earth's crust, which provide nutrients and warmth for the life that gathers there.
