Here Are Some Information About Shark

Evolution

Sharks have been around for over 400 million years, which is around 200 million years before dinosaurs.

Skeleton

Sharks are cartilaginous fish, which means their skeletons are made of cartilage instead of bone. Cartilage is lighter and more flexible than bone, which helps sharks make quick turns and stay afloat.

Size

Sharks can range in size from the dwarf lanternshark, which is only 17 centimeters long, to the whale shark, which can grow up to 12 meters long.

Predators

Most sharks are top predators that eat fish, seals, penguins, and other sharks. However, some larger species, like the basking shark and the whale shark, only eat plankton.

Senses

Sharks have three primary senses: smell, an electrical sense, and vision. They use smell to approach prey, then use their electrical sense to hunt more closely, and finally use vision at the last minute.

Electroreceptor organs

Sharks have small black spots near their eyes, nose, and mouth called ampullae of Lorenzini. These organs allow sharks to sense electromagnetic fields and temperature changes in the ocean.

Endangered

Many shark species are endangered, with some populations dropping by 71% in half a century. The main threats to sharks are overfishing, habitat loss, and ecosystem loss.

about us

Some sea life, called rift communities, live around cracks in the ocean floor called fissures. These cracks expose hot minerals from the Earth's crust, which provide nutrients and warmth for the life that gathers there.
