Here Are Some Information About Turtles:

Classification

Turtles are reptiles with a hard shell covering their body. They are cold-blooded vertebrates with scales covering their body.

Habitat

Turtles live in many parts of the world, including freshwater ponds, lakes, rivers, oceans, forests, and deserts.

Types

There are more than 350 species of turtles. Turtles that live on land are often called tortoises, while some water turtles are called terrapins.

Shell

Turtles have a hard, bony shell that protects them from predators. The shell is made of cartilage that grows from the ribs and wraps around the body. Turtles can feel through their shell because it has nerve endings.

Movement

Turtles are known for moving very slowly. Sea turtles have flippers instead of front feet, and are adapted for aquatic life.

Diet

Turtles eat worms, snails, insects, jellyfish, and shellfish. Some turtles, like green sea turtles, have serrated ridges along the inside of their beaks that simulate teeth.

Lifespan

Turtles are the planet's oldest reptile group, outliving snakes and crocodiles.

Other facts

Turtles can store food in the form of fat, and some can store water too. They can live for days or even weeks without having anything to eat or drink.

Some sea life, called rift communities, live around cracks in the ocean floor called fissures. These cracks expose hot minerals from the Earth's crust, which provide nutrients and warmth for the life that gathers there.
